Oldest Temples

Perched atop the scenic Gop Dungar (Gop Hill) in Jamjodhpur Taluka, the Gopnath Mahadev Temple is a hidden gem that blends ancient history, architectural intrigue, and breathtaking natural beauty. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered one of the oldest surviving stone temples in Gujarat.

જામજોધપુર તાલુકામાં આવેલા ગોપ ડુંગર પર બિરાજમાન ગોપનાથ મહાદેવનું મંદિર ગુજરાતના સૌથી પ્રાચીન મંદિરોમાંનું એક છે. છઠ્ઠી સદીમાં મૈત્રક કાળ દરમિયાન બનેલું આ મંદિર તેની અનોખી સ્થાપત્યકલા અને કુદરતી સૌંદર્ય માટે જાણીતું છે.

"Its calm atmosphere and scenic surroundings make it a wonderful spot for meditation and prayer — a must-visit for devotees of Lord Shiva."

The temple is approximately 28 kilometres from Lalpur and about 60 km from Jamnagar city. There are two ways to reach the top of the hill — either by stairs or by vehicle via a motorable road all the way to the top.

The Shivlinga here is particularly sacred — believed to be one of the oldest in Saurashtra, comparable in antiquity to Somnath. During Shravan Amavasya, thousands of devotees visit for darshan.

Itihas

Temple History

6th Century CE · Maitraka Period

The Gop Temple is established — one of the earliest surviving stone temples in Gujarat. The original structure features a square plan with pyramidal masonry roof, 23 feet high, built on the hilltop above the Vartu River.

Puranic Legend

After the Rasalila, the Gopis fashioned a Shivalinga from their own ornaments at Lord Krishna's suggestion to seek blessings from Mahadev. Thus the deity became known as Gopnath — "Lord of the Gopis."

15th Century

The Waja Rathod monarch of Zanzmer, Lagdhirsingh Rathod, grants 1,300 bighas of land to the temple. The Gohil royal family reconstructs the shrine following earlier damage.

16th Century

The temple withstands invasions. Local Hindu communities and the Rathore royals of Zanzmer unite to protect the shrine. The last major attack is repelled by a small but determined group of defenders.

Present Day

A living pilgrimage site rated 4.7/5 on Google Maps. Wind farms now surround the hill. Minimal entry fee of ₹10. Open 24 hours. Best visited at dawn, dusk, or during the monsoon season.

Nearest Jinavari Temple

Kan Kankri &
Jinavari

The area around Moti Gop is rich with ancient sacred sites. The Jinavari (Zinvari) village, also known as Nana or Juna Gop, lies just east of Gop village. This is the site of the famous Gop Sun Temple, dating to the same Maitraka period.

The "Kan Kankri" route along the Jamnagar–Porbandar highway passes through a corridor of ancient Shiva temples — from Bholeshwar Mahadev, through Gopnath Mahadev on Gop Hill, to Ashapura Mata and standing Lord Ganesha at Ghumli, and onward to Kileshwara Mahadev in the Barda Wildlife Reserve.

📍

Gop Sun Temple (Jinavari)

Zinavari village, Jamjodhpur Taluka — one of the earliest surviving stone temples in Gujarat, built on a hilltop on the banks of the Vartu River. The pyramidal masonry roof and arch-like gavaksha windows reflect 6th-century Maitraka craftsmanship.

Yatra Planning

Plan Your Visit

🕐

Temple Hours

Open 24 hours, 7 days a week, all year round. Best time: 5–7 AM for morning aarti, or 5–7 PM for sunset views. Monsoon season (July–September) is most scenic.

🛣️

How to Reach

Located ~60 km from Jamnagar, ~31 km from Bhanvad, ~125 km from Rajkot. Two routes to the hilltop — stairs (traditional) or motorable road for vehicles. Reachable by car or bike.

🎟️

Entry & Facilities

Minimal entry fee of ₹10. Facilities include hostel accommodation, cowshed (gaushala), and basic amenities. No large commercial development — a peaceful, simple pilgrimage.

🌿

Best Season

Monsoon (July–Sept) for lush green hills and mist. Winter (Oct–Feb) for clear skies and comfortable weather. Avoid peak summer heat. Shravan Amavasya draws the largest crowds.

🌅

Nearby Attractions

Wind farms adjacent to the hill offer dramatic photo opportunities. The Gop Sun Temple (Jinavari) is minutes away. Ghumli ruins with Ashapura Mata and standing Ganesh statue are en route.

🙏

Special Days

Shravan Amavasya (new moon in the holy month of Shravan) draws devotees in the thousands. Mahashivratri is another major festival. Regular Monday Shiva prayers draw local devotees weekly.
